My cause that was put into the WDD site was music. While musical opinions are something that definetly differe from person to person, I find that there are preconcieved biases that prevent people from exploring further sounds and genres that they might potentially find interesting. It all comes down to one statement: I think all ______ sounds the same. For this prep in making a convincing arguement, I will go with the misconception:

I think all rap music sounds the same.

Crafting a convincing argument comes from debunking previous beliefs, in which case the first step is to present the core facts. These facts must present the facts while not bringing up the myth being debunked. This prevents the Overkill Backfire Effect, which means that people only support the misconception even more dispite hearing counter evidence against it. In this case, I would bring ip facts about various sub-genres that exist within the overarching genre of "rap".
